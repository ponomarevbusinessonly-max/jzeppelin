## 2. Legal framework

We set and access information on your device in accordance with:

- **§ 25 of the German Telecommunications Digital Services Data Protection Act (TDDDG)** — formerly TTDSG, renamed on 14 May 2024 — which implements Article 5(3) of the ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC;
- **Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)**, where the information stored or accessed constitutes personal data.

Under § 25(1) TDDDG, storing information on your device or accessing information already stored there is only permitted with your prior consent, based on clear and comprehensive information — unless the storage or access is **strictly necessary** to provide the service you have explicitly requested (§ 25(2) TDDDG).

Non-compliance with § 25 TDDDG can result in fines of up to **€300,000** under § 28 TDDDG, and — where personal data is involved — up to 4% of global annual turnover under GDPR.

## 3. What are cookies and similar technologies?

**Cookies** are small text files placed on your device by a website you visit. They allow the site to remember your actions and preferences over time.

On this site, we also use **local storage** (a browser-based storage mechanism similar in effect to cookies) and, where you consent, **cookieless tracking techniques** operated by our hosting provider.

For the purposes of § 25 TDDDG, all of these count as "storing information on, or accessing information already stored in, the end user's terminal equipment" — and are therefore treated identically under this Policy.

## 4. What we use, and why

We categorise the technologies we use into two groups: **strictly necessary** (no consent required) and **analytics** (loaded only with your consent).

### 4.1 Strictly necessary — no consent required

These are required for the website to function correctly and to respect your cookie choices. They cannot be disabled through our cookie banner because the site would not work properly without them.

| Name | Type | Storage | Purpose | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `jzeppelin-cookie-consent` | Local storage (first-party) | Your browser | Stores your cookie preferences so the banner is not shown on every page load | 12 months or until you clear your browser storage |

**Legal basis:** § 25(2) No. 2 TDDDG — strictly necessary to provide the functionality you have requested (remembering your consent choice is itself a requirement of GDPR Art. 7(3)).

### 4.2 Analytics — only with your consent

We use **Vercel Web Analytics** and **Vercel Speed Insights**, provided by Vercel Inc., to understand how visitors use the site and to measure page performance. These are **cookieless** — Vercel identifies visitors via a daily-rotating hash derived from the incoming request, rather than through persistent identifiers. The hash is automatically discarded after 24 hours and cannot be used to re-identify you across days or across other websites.

Even though no traditional cookies are set, these tools still involve accessing information from your browser (via the native Web Vitals API and the incoming request), which is why we load them **only after you have given consent** through our cookie banner.

| Service | Provider | What is collected | Retention | Cookies set |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vercel Web Analytics | Vercel Inc., 340 S Lemon Ave #4133, Walnut, CA 91789, USA | Anonymised page views, referrer, country, OS, device, browser; daily-rotating visitor hash | Aggregated; individual hashes discarded after 24 hours | None |
| Vercel Speed Insights | Vercel Inc. (as above) | Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS, INP, TTFB), page URL, device type | Aggregated; no individual profile | None |

**Legal basis:** Your consent under § 25(1) TDDDG and Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR. You can withdraw consent at any time (see Section 6).

**International transfers:** Vercel Inc. is based in the United States. Data is transferred on the basis of the **EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework** (Vercel Inc. is self-certified) and, as an additional safeguard, **Standard Contractual Clauses** under Art. 46(2)(c) GDPR. You can review Vercel's privacy notice at [vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy](https://vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy).

## 6. How to manage your choices

### 6.1 When you first visit

A cookie banner appears on your first visit. You can:

- **Accept** — we load analytics tools (see Section 4.2);
- **Reject** — only strictly necessary storage (Section 4.1) is used;
- **Close the banner without choosing** — treated as rejection; no analytics are loaded.

Refusing consent is as easy as giving it, and both options are equally accessible, in line with EDPB guidance and the DSK guidance of November 2024.

### 6.2 Changing your mind later

You can change your choice at any time by:

1. Clearing the `jzeppelin-cookie-consent` entry in your browser's local storage, which will make the banner appear again on your next visit; **or**
2. Clearing all site data for jzeppelin.eu through your browser settings.

> **Note:** If the "Cookie Settings" link is added to the footer in a future release, it will be linked from this paragraph.

### 6.3 Browser-level controls

You can also block or delete cookies and local storage directly through your browser. Most browsers offer a "Do Not Track" signal, a "Global Privacy Control" setting, and a private/incognito mode that rejects non-essential storage by default. Guidance for common browsers:

- [Chrome](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647)
- [Firefox](https://support.mozilla.org/kb/cookies-information-websites-store-on-your-computer)
- [Safari](https://support.apple.com/guide/safari/manage-cookies-sfri11471/mac)
- [Edge](https://support.microsoft.com/microsoft-edge/delete-cookies-in-microsoft-edge)

Please note that blocking strictly necessary storage may prevent parts of the site from working correctly — for example, the cookie banner may re-appear on every page load.
