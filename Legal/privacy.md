# Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 17 April 2026

## 6. International data transfers

Some of our processors are located outside the European Economic Area. Transfers to third countries are subject to Art. 44–49 GDPR.

### 6.1 United States (Vercel Inc.)

The EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework (adequacy decision of 10 July 2023) permits transfers of personal data to U.S. organisations that are self-certified under the framework. Vercel Inc. is self-certified, which means the Commission has recognised that an adequate level of protection applies to data transferred to Vercel.

As an additional safeguard and in view of the evolving case law following *Schrems II* (CJEU, C-311/18), Vercel's Data Processing Agreement incorporates **Standard Contractual Clauses** (Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914) as a belt-and-braces measure. You can review Vercel's commitments at [vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy](https://vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy).

### 6.2 Sri Lanka (FormSubmit.co / Devro LABS)

Sri Lanka is **not** the subject of an adequacy decision by the European Commission. The FormSubmit.co privacy policy (last updated in 2019) does not incorporate Standard Contractual Clauses and does not offer a GDPR-compliant Data Processing Agreement.

Where you choose to submit your email via our "Notify me" form, that submission passes through FormSubmit.co's servers before reaching our inbox. We have identified this as a **compliance risk** and are in the process of replacing this service with an EU-hosted alternative.

**Until that migration is complete, your use of the "Notify me" form constitutes explicit consent under Art. 49(1)(a) GDPR** to the transfer of your email address (and any other fields you fill in) to Sri Lanka, with the understanding that the level of data protection there may not be equivalent to that within the EEA.

If you would prefer not to use the form, you can contact us directly at **info@jzeppelin.eu** instead, and your email will be handled entirely within the EU (via our email provider).

### 6.3 No other international transfers

We do not currently transfer personal data to any other third countries.

## 8. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

- **Right of access** (Art. 15) — you can ask for a copy of the data we hold about you.
- **Right to rectification** (Art. 16) — you can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
- **Right to erasure / "right to be forgotten"** (Art. 17) — you can ask us to delete your data, subject to legal exceptions.
- **Right to restriction of processing** (Art. 18) — you can ask us to limit how we use your data while a complaint is being resolved.
- **Right to data portability** (Art. 20) — you can ask for your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Right to object** (Art. 21) — you can object to processing based on legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)).
- **Right to withdraw consent** (Art. 7(3)) — where we rely on consent, you can withdraw it at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
- **Right not to be subject to automated decision-making** (Art. 22) — not applicable here, as we do not carry out such processing.

### How to exercise your rights

Send an email to **info@jzeppelin.eu** from the address associated with your data (or otherwise identifying yourself in a way that lets us verify the request). We will respond within one month, as required by Art. 12(3) GDPR. This is free of charge, unless your request is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

### Right to lodge a complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Art. 77 GDPR). The authority competent for UcanDrive GmbH is:

## 9. Security

We apply appropriate technical and organisational measures (Art. 32 GDPR) to protect personal data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction, including:

- HTTPS/TLS encryption for all traffic to and from the site;
- Access to admin systems limited to authorised staff with unique credentials and, where available, two-factor authentication;
- Use of reputable infrastructure providers (Vercel) who undergo independent security audits;
- Minimisation — we do not collect data we do not need.

No system is perfectly secure. If a data breach occurs that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours (Art. 33 GDPR) and, where the risk is high, notify affected individuals directly (Art. 34 GDPR).
